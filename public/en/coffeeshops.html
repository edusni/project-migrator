Amsterdam Coffeeshops Guide 2026

Everything tourists need to know: rules, prices, best locations, and common mistakes to avoid.

Good News: Unlike southern Netherlands, Amsterdam still allows tourists in coffeeshops. However, rules have changed significantly in 2025-2026.

What is a Coffeeshop?

A coffeeshop in the Netherlands is a licensed establishment where cannabis can be legally purchased and consumed. Despite the name, they're not primarily about coffee (though most serve drinks).

Important: A "coffee shop" (two words) serves coffee. A "coffeeshop" (one word) sells cannabis. Don't confuse them!

First-Timer Tips

  • Start slow: Dutch weed is strong (20-30% THC). Half a joint is enough for most first-timers.
  • Ask the staff: Tell them your experience level. They'll recommend appropriate strains.
  • Indica vs Sativa: Indica = relaxing ("in-da-couch"). Sativa = energetic, creative.
  • Edibles warning: Effects take 1-2 hours. Don't eat more thinking "it's not working."
  • Drink water: Cottonmouth is real. Stay hydrated.
  • Don't mix: Alcohol + cannabis = bad idea, especially for beginners.

Coffeeshop Etiquette

  • Buy something if you're going to sit and smoke
  • Don't bring outside cannabis into a coffeeshop
  • Photography is usually prohibited or requires permission
  • Be respectful of staff and other customers
  • Clean up after yourself
