Where to Stay in Amsterdam 2026

I'll tell you EXACTLY where to stay based on your profile, budget and what you want to do. No vague "it depends" talk.

The Taxes Are Absurd:
  • Tourist tax: 12.5% on TOP of everything
  • VAT: 21% (in effect since 2025)
  • Total: Over 33% TAX on base price

That €100/night hotel actually costs €133+. Budget accordingly!

Best Neighborhoods by Profile

Classic Choice

Jordaan

Best for: Romance, photography, canal views, first-timers

Price range: €150-300/night

The Instagram Amsterdam. Charming canals, cafes, boutiques. Walking distance to Anne Frank House. Most expensive but worth it for the experience.

Cons: Touristy, expensive restaurants, busy on weekends.

Local Favorite

De Pijp

Best for: Food lovers, nightlife, young travelers, Albert Cuyp market

Price range: €100-200/night

The "Brooklyn of Amsterdam." Diverse, trendy, great restaurants and bars. Albert Cuyp street market is unmissable.

Cons: South of center, some areas noisy at night.

Budget Smart

Noord

Best for: Budget travelers, modern vibe, NDSM, A'DAM Lookout

Price range: €80-150/night

Across the IJ river. Industrial-chic, creative spaces, free ferry to central. Much cheaper than central locations.

Cons: Requires ferry or metro, less traditional feel.

Convenience

Centrum

Best for: Short stays, first-timers wanting walkability

Price range: €180-400/night

Everything within walking distance. Central Station, Dam Square, main attractions. Most touristy but most convenient.

Cons: Crowded, loud, tourist traps everywhere, expensive.

Hidden Gem

Oost (East)

Best for: Parks, multicultural dining, Artis Zoo, families

Price range: €100-180/night

Residential but well-connected. Near Oosterpark and Tropical Museum. Great Indonesian and Surinamese food.

Cons: Less central, fewer attractions nearby.

Airbnb: Almost Dead in Amsterdam

The Reality:

  • Maximum 30 nights/year per property (soon 15 in center)
  • Needs specific license - many listings are illegal
  • Fines up to €21,000 for illegal rentals
  • You might get kicked out mid-stay if authorities check

Hotels are often the safer choice when you factor in the risks.

Money-Saving Tips

  • Stay in Noord or Oost: 30-40% cheaper than Centrum/Jordaan
  • Book 2-3 months ahead: Prices spike closer to dates
  • Avoid weekends: Prices jump 20-30% Fri-Sun
  • Consider hostels with private rooms: Often cheaper than budget hotels
  • Look for "stay 3 nights" deals: Many hotels offer discounts
